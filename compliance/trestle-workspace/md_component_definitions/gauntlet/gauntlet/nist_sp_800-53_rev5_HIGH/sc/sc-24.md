# SC-24 — Fail in Known State

## Control Statement

The system fails to a known secure state in the event of system failure, preserving system state information in failure and protecting the system from unauthorized use following a failure. The known state must be defined, documented, and tested.

Key implementation points:
- The GauntletSystemAlert CRD is the primary mechanism for entering a known degraded state; when the controller encounters conditions such as a SIEM export failure, HMAC verification failure, FIPS self-test failure, or bootstrap check failure, it creates a GauntletSystemAlert and transitions its internal state machine to `Degraded`, halting all new probe Job scheduling until the alert is acknowledged and resolved by an authorized operator
- In degraded state, the controller continues to process and verify probe results that are already in-flight (preserving their evidence value) but does not schedule new Jobs, ensuring that the cluster is not silently assessed as compliant during a period when the assessment pipeline's integrity is in question; all degraded-state transitions are exported to the SIEM with a structured reason code
- Contingency recovery targets an RTO of 4 hours and RPO of the last Helm values backup; Velero is used to back up all Gauntlet CRDs (GauntletProbeResult, GauntletIncident, GauntletSystemAlert, GauntletAOAuthorization) on a defined schedule, ensuring that in a full cluster recovery scenario, historical assessment evidence and active alert state can be restored to the known-good point before the failure

## Evidence Produced

- GauntletSystemAlert CRs with reason code, onset timestamp, and resolution timestamp
- SIEM event records for each state transition (Active → Degraded → Resolved) with structured metadata

## Customer Responsibility

The agency must configure Velero or equivalent backup tooling for the gauntlet-system namespace CRDs, define a runbook for GauntletSystemAlert response within their 4-hour RTO target, and test the degraded-state recovery procedure at least annually.
