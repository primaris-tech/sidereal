# AU-11 — Audit Record Retention

## Control Statement

Retain audit records for an organization-defined time period to provide support for
after-the-fact investigations of security incidents and to meet regulatory and
organizational information retention requirements.

## Gauntlet Implementation

Gauntlet enforces a two-tier retention model with minimums derived from the
operator-configured FIPS 199 impact level (`global.impactLevel`).

### Tier 1: In-Cluster Retention

`GauntletProbeResult` resources have a configurable TTL enforced by the controller.
The controller enforces an **impact-level-dependent minimum TTL** — values below the
threshold are rejected at validation:

| Impact Level | Minimum In-Cluster TTL | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| High | 365 days | FedRAMP High baseline online retention |
| Moderate | 365 days | FedRAMP Moderate baseline online retention |
| Low | 180 days | Reduced retention consistent with Low risk |

The TTL is configured via `spec.auditRetention.minTTLDays` in the Gauntlet Helm
values. Operators may increase this value but cannot decrease it below the floor
for their selected impact level.

### Tier 2: SIEM Long-Term Retention

All `GauntletProbeResult` records are exported to the configured SIEM target in the
operator-selected format (JSON, CEF, LEEF, Syslog RFC 5424, or OCSF). The SIEM
is responsible for long-term retention. Minimum SIEM retention per impact level:

| Impact Level | Minimum SIEM Retention | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| High | 3 years | FedRAMP High baseline (1yr online + 2yr offline) |
| Moderate | 3 years | FedRAMP Moderate baseline |
| Low | 1 year | Reduced retention consistent with Low risk |

In-cluster records and SIEM records are maintained independently. SIEM unavailability
does not affect in-cluster retention, and in-cluster TTL expiry does not affect SIEM
records.

### Retention Monitoring

The controller monitors in-cluster record counts and surfaces a Prometheus metric
(`gauntlet_audit_record_count`) for capacity monitoring. Export failure metrics
alert operators if SIEM records are not being created, preventing silent retention gaps.

## Evidence Produced

- Helm values schema enforcing 365-day minimum TTL floor
- Controller validation rejecting TTL values below minimum
- `gauntlet_audit_record_count` Prometheus metric
- SIEM export failure metric and alerting

## Customer Responsibility

The deploying agency must:
1. Configure their SIEM retention policy to retain Gauntlet records for a minimum of
   3 years total
2. Document the retention configuration in their SSP as the AU-11 implementation
3. Monitor `gauntlet_audit_export_failures` metric to detect SIEM retention gaps
4. Verify SIEM retention configuration during annual control assessments
