# AU-10 — Non-Repudiation

## Control Statement

Provide irrefutable evidence that an individual (or process acting on behalf of an
individual) has performed a specific action.

## Gauntlet Implementation

Non-repudiation is enforced through two complementary mechanisms: individual principal
requirements and a cryptographic audit chain.

### Individual Principal Requirements

All security-sensitive actions in Gauntlet require an authenticated, individual
Kubernetes RBAC principal. Shared service accounts are rejected at admission for
actions where non-repudiation is required:

- Transitioning execution mode (`executionMode: observe` or `enforce`) — requires `gauntlet-live-executor` role
- Creating AO authorizations — requires `gauntlet-approver` role
- Acknowledging `GauntletSystemAlert` — individual principal required; shared service
  accounts rejected by admission enforcement policy
- Modifying security-relevant configuration — requires `gauntlet-security-override` role

Every action above is recorded with the Kubernetes username from the request context.
Because Gauntlet requires individual accounts (not shared), this username maps to a
specific person documented in the agency's access management records.

### Cryptographic Audit Chain

The HMAC signing chain from probe execution to audit record provides cryptographic
non-repudiation for probe results:

1. Controller generates a unique HMAC key per execution — recorded with the execution
2. Probe runner signs the result with this key — establishes that this specific probe
   runner produced this specific result
3. Controller verifies the signature before recording — any alteration is detected
4. Record is written to append-only `GauntletProbeResult` — cannot be modified
5. Record is exported to SIEM with payload signature — transit integrity verified

This chain ensures that a recorded `GauntletProbeResult` irrefutably reflects what
the probe runner observed, when it observed it, and that it has not been altered.

### Acknowledgment Records

`GauntletSystemAlert` acknowledgment records capture:
- Kubernetes username (individual, not shared)
- Timestamp of acknowledgment
- Stated remediation action
- Alert type and context

These records are exported to the SIEM and cannot be modified after creation.

## Evidence Produced

- Kubernetes RBAC role definitions requiring individual principals for sensitive actions
- Admission enforcement policy rejecting shared service account acknowledgments
- HMAC verification status in every GauntletProbeResult record
- GauntletSystemAlert acknowledgment records in SIEM export

## Customer Responsibility

The deploying agency must:
1. Ensure all Gauntlet operator accounts are individual (not shared) per agency AC-2 procedures
2. Maintain identity records mapping Kubernetes usernames to specific individuals
3. Retain these records for the duration required by their non-repudiation policy
